Youth

Our program exists to help our youth experience and glorify God by acquiring knowledge of His Word, providing opportunities to care for one another, and reaching out to our neighbors in need.

Church membership is not required to participate in youth programs. Contact Sarah Maslowski, Director of Youth Ministries to get on her email list to get most current updates, or with any questions for our programs for youth grades 6-12.

During the School Year:

Confirmation for grades 6-9 focuses on building community with on another and the church while discovering more about Christ, growing in faith and being in a safe place to ask questions. Includes worship, small groups, service projects and speakers. Meets on Wednesday evenings, 6:30-7:15 pm.

Imagine encourages high school students, grades 9-12, to continue their faith journey in a welcoming and supportive environment. Includes worship, small groups, service projects and speakers. Meets on Wednesday evenings, 6:30-7:15 pm.

Crossroads promotes faith, fun and relationships and include events and activities such as a fall corn maze, a winter broomball game, and paintball in the spring. Open to all youth and friends, grades 6-12.

Jubilation is a vocal choir for grades 6-12 that sings during worship one Sunday a month and at one service time on holidays. They also perform Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat both locally and on a summer tour. Rehearsals are Wednesdays, 7:15-8:30 pm immediately following Confirmation and Imagine. All 6-12 graders are welcome. No singing experience is needed.

Fishtown Band is a youth band for grades 9-12 that plays in various worship services throughout the year. Members are accepted by director's approval. Director is also our Director of Youth, Sarah Maslowski. Rehearsals are Wednesdays, 4:00-5:00 pm.

During the Summer:

Summer Crossroads is an extension of regular Crossroads which provides an opportunity to stay connected during the summer months. Youth gather for a 3-day mini-camp, 8:00 am-5:00 pm. Each day includes devotions, a service project, lunch, afternoon fun and ice cream. Open to all middle school youth and friends, going into grades 6-8. High School youth help as leaders.

Mission Trip is an opportunity for high school youth, grades 9-12, to connect to each other, to communities, and to God while on a life-changing, Christ-centered mission. Trips typically last a week. Locations and dates change each year.

Camp WAPO is a Lake Wapogasset Lutheran Bible Camp that St. Stephen youth attend every summer for a life-changing week-long experience that discovers Jesus Christ through Christian community in an outdoor ministry setting. Attend Youth Camp (completed grades 7-9) or Servant Leadership Program at Ox Lake (completed grades 9-12). Usually attends in late July / early August.

Adults

Adult ministries focus on the common purpose of relationship building while discovering and growing in the possibilities of an abundant life in Christ.

At St. Stephen we offer many opportunities to connect as we explore our faith and ask questions together. From fellowship and educational opportunities to helping others in need, there is sure to be something that will appeal to you.

Adult Education helps us explore a variety of topics for faith-filled life-long learning. Opportunities are offered throughout the year. Learn about the Bible, join in conversations around current community challenges and much more! All are welcome to explore with us!

Book Group Like to read? Like to talk about what you read? All are welcome to a book discussion with St. Stephen leaders!

Bible Study at 7500 York is offered at 7500 York Senior Living in the Social Room and are led by St. Stephen leaders. All are welcome! Residency is not required to attend.

Moms Spirituality Life Group Moms of school age children or younger learn ways to integrate the presence of God in our daily lives through meditation, spiritual activities and discussions with friends.

Quilters This group of women gathers weekly to make quilts. Quilts are later given to many charitable organizations that help people in need.

St. Stephen Annual Luncheon We gather to enjoy a Swedish Meatball lunch and program. All ages are welcome!

Tavern Topic Night This adult Bible Study is held monthly in a fun, informal setting at NorthStar Tavern in Bloomington. Enjoy food and beverages and conversation while meeting new or old friends. All are welcome!

Tuesday Morning Men are a group of retired men in mission building with faith. They help with building and maintenance issues as they arise.

Women's Ministry Women gather to grow in body, spirit and mind.

Support Groups

Addiction Prevention and Recovery

MISSION: To develop and manage an action plan for St. Stephen which will educate and provide hope for recovery (healing) to individuals and families suffering from the wounds of addiction. Consistent with the theme of wellness, the plan will also seek to educate members about ways to identify and prevent addictive behaviors.

Caregiver Support

For those who care for someone else who is sick, disabled, or elderly. The number of caregivers is steadily growing within our society and congregation. One contributing factor is that people overall are living longer in conjunction with advancement of medical treatments.

St. Stephen has identified the growing population and the need for support of caregivers and offers a Caregiver Support Group. The group typically gathers every other Tuesday, 1:30-3:00 pm at church. This is an open group and all caregivers are welcome to attend. The gatherings are facilitated by our Faith Community Nurse.

Grief Support

Grief is the pain we feel when we lose someone very special to us. Everyone experiences grief at different times in life. Too often we grieve silently and alone.

Fortunately there are resources for help and support. Because God created us to be in community where we can recieve comfort, support and love, St. Stephen offers opportunities for you and your family as you grieve the loss of a loved one.

Wellness & Healing

As Children of God we are called to Love the Lord our God with all our heart and soul and might and love our neighbors as ourselves. When we are good stewards of our well-being, we can more easily live out our purpose in God’s creation…loving God and loving our neighbor in our own unique way.
